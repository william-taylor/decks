Case Study

Going FAST - When Scrum is slowing you down

Background

Author: Per Beining an Agile consultant, joined a financial services firm as a Scrum Master for two teams and transitioned into a Project Manager.

Goal: Move a system from private to public cloud. Reimplementing features and configuraiton so it could be run on a a public cloud platform.

Background

Progress: The project had been running for more than a year, and the product owner and wider team showed high levels of competence.

Problem: Nothing had been released to users and they were 6 weeks away from their first deadline. As such they looked to alternative methods to get back on track.

  • Amount of remaining work compared to time left
  • Too long iterations in combination with new things were learned on a daily basis
  • The competence pool was split between two separate teams

Class Discussion

What should the organisation do?

Solution

Moved to the FAST Agile Framework after getting approval from the Product Owner giving the wider group a 1 hour introduction.

Organising into a single collective with an agreement in place, moved to short 2 day value cycles, trusted people to own their work.

Outcome

They met their deadline and the system was launched with real users. The short value cycles enabled the project to pivot where needed and meet the operational needs of the system while also working on new features.

Their success saw a move in the wider organisation. They took a poll to see if people wanted to go back to scrum, 9 were in favour of FAST and none against.
